delegation is the fundamental economic act of scaling human output
what happens when cognitive labor becomes infinitely delegable
cost drops to near zero
constraint shifts to articulation and judgment

historical arc
pre-industrial delegation was slavery servitude apprenticeship
high coordination costs limited by physical presence
industrial era delegation equals employment
org charts management hierarchies taylor scientific management
factory as delegation machine
knowledge economy delegation equals specialization plus outsourcing
you dont do your own taxes legal accounting
transaction costs coase determine firm boundaries
ai era delegation equals prompting
cost of delegating cognitive task approaches cost of describing it

each era new forms of leverage new constraints

coasean boundary dissolves
coase theory of the firm
companies exist because internal coordination cheaper than market transactions
when agents execute at near zero marginal cost calculation flips
why employ team when you can delegate to agents on demand
firm becomes mission specification engine not labor coordination engine
see synthetic organizations article

middle layer compresses
middle management exists to translate strategy into execution
aggregate information upward
if agents do both translation and aggregation what remains
judgment calls that cant be specified
relationship management trust politics culture
novel problem framing
middle doesnt disappear gets radically compressed

leverage becomes unlimited for individuals
single person can orchestrate
research teams agent researchers
content production agent writers editors
customer service agent support
operations agent schedulers coordinators
solo operator with agent leverage competes with teams
one person unicorn becomes structurally possible
but constraint shifts to persons capacity for specification and judgment

new scarcities when delegation is cheap what becomes valuable

articulation
ability to specify what you want with enough precision that agents can execute
prompt engineering is trivial version
real articulation goals constraints success criteria edge cases values
people who articulate well become new bottleneck
ill know it when i see it becomes expensive
iteration costs attention

judgment
agents execute humans decide whats worth executing
prioritization taste ethics strategy remain human
market will increasingly pay for judgment not execution

attention
even with infinite delegation human attention finite
can only review so many outputs make so many decisions
attention becomes ultimate scarce resource
previously attention scarce because content abundant
now attention scarce because work is abundant

delegation paradox
more delegation capacity
more things attempted
more review required
attention exhausted
quality drops
infinite leverage without infinite attention creates ceiling

solutions
hierarchical delegation agents reviewing agents
requires trust infrastructure see trust stack article
taste compression specifying preferences so tightly review becomes sampling
outcome orientation measure results not process

economic implications

labor markets
tasks that can be specified cleanly price collapses to agent marginal cost
tasks requiring judgment relationship novelty price premium grows
premium on human widens
most current jobs are mixtures
job doesnt disappear it fragments
specification parts get delegated judgment parts get concentrated
wage polarization accelerates
people who orchestrate earn more
people who did delegatable parts transition or exit

pricing models
shift from time based hours salaries to outcome based deliverables results
when execution cheap charging for execution time makes no sense
value is in specification and judgment hard to price directly
rise of outcome as a service
pay for leads not hours
code deployed not developer time

capital allocation
human capital valuable because training took years execution capacity limited
with delegation value shifts to system that enables delegation
capital flows to agent infrastructure the plumbing
data moats agents need information
trust reputation systems delegation requires confidence
specification tools helping humans articulate

inequality
early delegation advantage compounds
those who can orchestrate agents early capture disproportionate value
familiar from every technology wave but speed different
question will ai tools lower barrier to orchestration fast enough to distribute gains

organizational response

changes competitive dynamics
incumbents face more agile competitors

management becomes curation
managers job shifts from directing humans to curating agent outputs
performance reviews become output audits
team meetings become system reviews
hiring becomes agent selection and configuration

culture becomes specification
company culture is unwritten specification of how we do things
with agents culture must become explicit enough to encode
we value quality over speed becomes specific rubrics gates evaluation criteria
companies that can articulate their culture win

career advice in delegation era
develop articulation skills can you specify what you want clearly
develop judgment can you evaluate outputs make good decisions
understand the tools not coding but orchestration
focus on non delegatable relationships trust novel problems
build attention discipline your attention is scarcest resource
