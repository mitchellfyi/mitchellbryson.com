What to implement

- Document intake plus validation: Agents pre-flight Property Information Forms, ID AML packs, mortgage docs. They chase missing fields and validate against known datasets, logging everything to a case ledger
- Search orchestration: A search conductor agent requests local authority, water, environmental searches early. It tracks SLAs and escalates when turnaround times slip
- Title QA and requisition drafting: A review agent cross-checks title, lease terms, exceptions. It drafts standardized Land Registry requisition responses for human sign-off
- Chain tracker and nudger: A coordinator agent maintains a privacy-safe chain state, nudging counterparties when dependencies unblock, with escalation paths to humans

Why it helps: Typical timelines, 12-16 weeks, often 120-160 days, are dominated by waiting and rework. Automating intake and chase-downs compresses elapsed time. Standardizing requisitions reduces back-and-forth. Proactive nudging shortens dead time.

Hiring from inboxes to a governed matching pipeline

The hardest parts of recruiting, discovery, triage, scheduling, are structured enough for agents, so long as fairness and explainability are designed in.

What to implement

- Profile and job normalization: Agents convert CVs and job descriptions into a common skills ontology, deduplicating and extracting verified evidence. Projects, certifications
- Discovery and shortlisting: A sourcing agent proposes shortlists by skill match and signal quality, with transparent rationales and bias checks. A human gate approves outreach
- Scheduling and comms: A coordinator agent handles availability, screening prompts, reminders. All interactions log to a candidate evidence trail
- Offer and pre-boarding checks: Agents generate offers from templates, trigger background Right-to-Work flows, watch for stall signals

Why it helps: UK time to hire typically runs 5 weeks, with time to fill around 42-48 days in many contexts. Recruiters lose 18 hours per vacancy to admin. Automating the handoffs compresses cycle time and frees humans for judgment.

Accounting close and reconciliation declare everything, automate the rest

Closing is 80% matching and proving. Agents can do the heavy lifting while humans set policy and investigate exceptions.

What to implement

- Bank GL reconciliation agent: Continuously matches bank feeds to sub-ledgers, flags variances with suggested reasons. Timing, coding, FX. Auto-posts only within tight tolerances
- Intercompany and accrual assistant: Suggests accruals and eliminates intercompany mismatches with evidence links and approval lanes
- Close orchestrator: A runbook agent sequences tasks, chases owners, blocks sign-off until controls are satisfied. It outputs a clean audit trail

Why it helps: Median monthly close times hover around 6 plus days, while leaders achieve 3-4.5 days by standardizing and automating reconciliations. Precisely where well-supervised agents fit.

What is next and why it matters

Insurance claims triage to coverage to settlement

Use agents at first notice of loss to collect evidence, estimate damage, verify coverage, route fraud-risk cases to specialists. Settle straightforward claims programmatically. Large insurers now report measurable cycle-time reductions from multi-model deployments. Signals that the operating model works when paired with human oversight.

Construction ops RFIs, submittals, schedule risk

Construction productivity has lagged the broader economy for years. Document-heavy tasks like RFIs, submittals, change orders are ideal for agents that draft, route, track approvals. A planning agent monitors schedule risk and nudges procurement.

Healthcare admin referrals, coding, prior auth

Administrative friction burdens patients and staff alike. Agents can pre-populate referrals, summarize notes for coding, assemble prior-auth packets with citations. Always with strict privacy controls and human gates.

Public-sector back-office forms to APIs

Legacy systems and fragmented data are a known drag. Start by turning forms into APIs with agents that validate, de-duplicate, route cases. A controller agent enforces policy and budget caps. Government auditors point to legacy tech as a core constraint. Digitisation programmes are now explicitly targeting this gap.

Governance guardrails the boring bits that make it safe

- Human gates at the right moments: brand legal comms, offer letters, contract terms, irreversible ledger postings
- Budgets and caps: per-route token and vendor spend ceilings with alerts and hard trips
- Policies as code: allow-lists, schema validation, PII redaction, fairness checks for hiring. Explicit case-law and regulator guidance embedded for conveyancing and claims
- Evidence ledger: tamper-evident logs of inputs, outputs, costs, approvals, model prompt versions. The antidote to black box fears
